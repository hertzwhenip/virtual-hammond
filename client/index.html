Hammond Organ

A Hammond organ is a musical instrument created in the last century (around 1937) to be played in churches. However, it ended up being one of the most important organs in the Rock, Jazz and Blues musical industry. Internally, it is composed of rotative cylinders that are able to produce nearly pure sounds, thus why they were named oscillators. The instrument allows to using up to nine oscillators at the same time, each one producing a different frequency that drifts according to the note played by the interpreter. The outcome is the sum of the nine frequencies. In particular, an oscillator will play a frequency given by the note and the next ones will be multiples of the first.
